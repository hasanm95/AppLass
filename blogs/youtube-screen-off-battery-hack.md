## The Science of Saving Juice: Why "True Black" Matters

If you own a modern smartphone (like a Samsung Galaxy, Google Pixel, or high-end OnePlus), you likely have an AMOLED or OLED display. Unlike traditional LCDs that use a constant backlight, OLED pixels are self-emissive—meaning they produce their own light.

When an OLED pixel displays "True Black," it **physically turns off**. It consumes **zero power**.

> **Expert Insight:** According to data presented at the Android Dev Summit, using dark modes or black overlays can reduce display power consumption by up to 63% on AMOLED screens at maximum brightness.

By using an app like ScreenVeil to overlay a pure black layer while you stream, you aren't just "dimming" the lights—you're essentially putting your hardware into a high-efficiency hibernation mode.
